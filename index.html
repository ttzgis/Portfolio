Selected Project 01 Global Volcanic Hazard Modelling

Project Overview

This project explored volcanic hazards on a global scale by combining eruption history, topography and atmospheric controls into a reproducible GIS workflow. The goal was to derive spatially explicit hazard zones for active volcanoes and to create a consistent basis for comparing different eruption environments worldwide.

Flow-Hazard Modelling

Lava flows, pyroclastic density currents and lahars were modelled as terrain-dependent flow hazards in ArcGIS Pro ModelBuilder using Distance Accumulation. The workflow iterated through active volcanoes, clipped a merged 90 m DEM within a 60 km radius, derived slope and aspect, converted slope into a calibrated cost raster and used a 2 km source buffer together with water barriers to simulate realistic downslope movement.

Ashfall and Inputs

Monthly ashfall scenarios for a 2 cm deposit thickness were estimated from wind direction, wind speed and the maximum VEI observed at each volcano. Different plume heights were linked to eruption intensity, which made it necessary to work with wind information from both the 950 hPa and 500 hPa levels. The modelling framework was supported by volcano coordinates and VEI history from Smithsonian and NOAA, a merged ALOS and SRTM DEM, and OSM water-body data used as a barrier layer.
